Tip 1: The Setup

  • Hold the camera as far away from your body as possible.
  • Try using a selfie stick, tripod, or even asking a stranger to take the photo.
  • Having the phone farther away will help prevent distortion and generally yields better photos
    • .

    Tip 2: Lighting

  • Make sure the light is not too harsh or too dim.
  • Taking photos between 10am and 3pm usually means the light will be too harsh unless you find a protected area where the sunlight doesn’t fall directly on you.

    • Tip 3: Angle

  • If you take photos after dusk you might need some external light sources to keep your face well-lit and the image clear.
  • Take the photo from a higher angle. Higher angles are preferred by many and when done well, look more natural.

    • Tip 4: Posture

  • Just don’t hold the camera too high. Keep your poses natural and unforced.
  • Most communication that occurs between people is nonverbal, and for that reason we’ve become adept at figuring out what’s natural and what’s staged.

    • Tip 5: Others

    Try to keep your poses as relaxed and natural as possible, as it’s noticeable when a pose is stiff and forced.

    Look for symmetry. The human brain has been proven to favor patterns and symmetry. Playing into that tendency often yields more visually appealing photos. For example, placing the subject of the image next to a reflective surface often produces interesting and relaxed photos.

    Push-up Variations

    Introduction to Push-ups

    The push-up is a classic compound exercise that works multiple muscles in just two movements, down and up. It can also target the chest, triceps and shoulders. However, over the years there are even more variations that can aid other muscle groups as well. Check out the list below.

    Variations

    Standard Military Pushup


    Go all the way down and all the way up, locking scapula. As "easy" as it is.

    Decline Pushup


    This variation works the upper chest and is much more difficult. This is because you now no longer have your legs to support your weight.

    Cobra Pushup


    This unique variation originates from India. It works mostly the triceps this time, rather than the chest, as usual. The pause also makes it harder than the regular variant, because of longer exposure to tension as your triceps dictate the movement.

    Archer Pushup


    As we progress into a more challenging variation, archer pushup really takes it to another level. It's almost similar to feeling only 1 arm doing the work the whole time!

    One-arm Pushup


    And here we are, one of the most difficult pushup variations to boot! For more advanced levels, you can make the exercise even harder. Beware of muscle imbalance, though, because you only make one arm put in the work!
